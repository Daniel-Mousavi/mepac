The agenda for Michigan’s economic future.

Ten fights that decide whether Michigan is a place where anyone can build, work, and grow. Concrete, pro-growth, and fair.

What We’re Fighting For

A pro-growth agenda for every Michigander.

We back leaders and policies that make it faster, fairer, and more affordable to build a business and a life in Michigan, and we hold the line against rules that lock people out.

01

Put People’s Skills to Work

Skilled Michiganders, including professionals trained abroad, shouldn’t be locked out of the work they’re qualified to do.

  • Recognize licenses earned in other states, and build real pathways for foreign-trained professionals.
  • Replace unnecessary licenses with simple registration where there’s no real safety risk.
  • Require a licensing decision within 30 days.
02

Cut the Red Tape, and Level the Field

Opening and running a business should be fast, affordable, and fair, and the rules shouldn’t be written by big incumbents to box out their competition.

  • Set hard deadlines on permits: a decision in a fixed window, or it’s approved.
  • Apply rules evenly across similar businesses (no separated kitchen for a hookah lounge when a cigarette shop needs none), and sunset the ones that no longer make sense.
  • Stop established players from using regulation to block smaller competitors.
03

Open the Door to Capital

Good businesses shouldn’t die for lack of a loan, and that gap falls hardest on the entrepreneurs banks overlook.

  • Expand loan guarantees and community lending for underserved businesses.
  • Strengthen the community lenders (CDFIs) that reach overlooked neighborhoods.
  • Offer competitive grants tied to real results: jobs, survival, growth.
04

Everyone’s Right to Build a Business Here

No one should be barred from owning property or running a business in Michigan because of where they come from.

  • Oppose any Michigan law restricting property or business ownership by national origin.
  • Protect the right of immigrant-owned businesses to operate and grow.
  • Treat this as a line we won’t let the state cross.
05

Affordable, Reliable Power

Michigan businesses and families deserve energy that’s cheap, dependable, and abundant.

  • Build more power of every kind: advanced nuclear, geothermal, solar, and wind.
  • Fix the grid and clear the backlog that stalls new energy projects.
  • Approve energy infrastructure fast.
06

Build Michigan’s Backbone

A modern economy runs on modern infrastructure, physical and digital.

  • Bring reliable high-speed internet to every business.
  • Invest in the roads and freight corridors that move Michigan goods.
  • Upgrade Michigan’s ports and harbors and grow our waterfronts.
07

Invest in Our Communities, and Build More Housing

Strong neighborhoods are where strong businesses grow, and workers need to afford to live where the jobs are.

  • Direct capital and investment into communities that have been overlooked.
  • Legalize more housing so growth reaches the whole state.
  • Let people build: end the delays that hold back good, code-compliant projects.
08

Open Real Paths to Opportunity

Every Michigander should have a clear route to a good job, and Michigan’s talent should stay in Michigan.

  • Expand employer-led training and apprenticeships tied to real hiring.
  • Build portable credentials that count across employers, with multiple routes to a career.
  • Attract and keep skilled talent here.
09

Help Businesses Modernize and Build What’s Next

Survival means keeping up, and Michigan should be where the next generation of industry gets built.

  • Help small businesses adopt the best modern tools, including AI.
  • Back research that turns into real Michigan products and companies.
  • Use Michigan’s purchasing power to pull next-generation industries here.
10

Honest, Open, Accountable Government

Government should work for the people who build the economy, out in the open, and free of corruption.

  • Publish what government does, and enforce anti-corruption hard.
  • Protect your property from eminent-domain abuse: no seizing your land to hand it to a developer.
  • Back an independent watchdog that tells the public the real cost of legislation before it passes.
